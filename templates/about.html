üéØ Project Overview

This diabetes risk prediction application uses machine learning to assess the likelihood of diabetes based on key health indicators. The model was trained on the Pima Indians Diabetes Dataset and employs a Gradient Boosting Classifier for accurate predictions.

üî¨ Model Performance

Our Gradient Boosting model achieved excellent performance metrics, making it reliable for diabetes risk assessment.

F1-Score 81% Balance between precision and recall
ROC-AUC 82.2% Model's discrimination ability

üîç Key Features

The model analyzes eight important health indicators:

  • Glucose Level: Most important predictor - blood sugar concentration
  • BMI: Body Mass Index indicating weight status
  • Age: Risk increases with age
  • Pregnancies: Number of pregnancies (for women)
  • Blood Pressure: Diastolic blood pressure measurement
  • Skin Thickness: Triceps skin fold thickness
  • Insulin: Serum insulin level
  • Diabetes Pedigree Function: Family history factor
